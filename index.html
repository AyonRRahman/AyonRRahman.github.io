Hello! 👋 I am Md Raqibur Rahman, seeking a PhD position in robotics, AI, or intelligent systems.

I study intelligent systems, computer vision, and robotics, focusing on building practical tools for real-world applications. My research interests include:
  • Real-time video analytics for industrial and high-intensity work environments, including object detection, activity monitoring, and efficiency feedback systems.
  • Autonomous navigation and mapping algorithms for underwater and aerial robots, including Visual SLAM, visual-inertial localization, and depth/ego-motion learning.
  • AI and machine learning applications for predictive modeling, anomaly detection, and efficiency optimization in industrial and maritime systems.
Currently, I am working as a Lecturer at North South University and as a Product Research Engineer at The Data Island, gaining hands-on experience in AI and robotics projects.

I completed my Master’s in Marine and Maritime Intelligent Robotics at NTNU and Université de Toulon, where my thesis focused on self-supervised scale-consistent depth and ego-motion learning from monocular video for underwater robots. My undergraduate research at Islamic University of Technology explored predictive modeling of ships’ estimated time of arrival using neural networks.

I am highly motivated, with strong academic performance and practical experience in robotics, AI, and computer vision. I am eager to contribute to cutting-edge research as a PhD student.
